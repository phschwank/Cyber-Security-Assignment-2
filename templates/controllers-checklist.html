1.

Make sure your employees know how their personal data is used and what their rights are under GDPR.

Make sure your employees are aware how their personal data is applied and what their rights are based on GDPR.

Specification — What is 'Personal Data'?

Personal data is information that relates to an identified or identifiable individual. Personal data only includes information relating to natural persons who:

  • can be identified or who are identifiable, directly from the information in question; or
  • who can be indirectly identified from that information in combination with other information.
  • list of identifiers, including:
    • name;
    • identification number;
    • location data; and
    • an online identifier.

2.

Do you have a process or SOPs to map or visualize data flows in your organisation?

Recommendation is missing.

Specification — Specific Subtitle?

You should perform an audit across your entire organisation or within particular business areas. It is recommended to choose a person with an in-depth knowledge of your business to do this task. This allows you to identify the data you process and how it flows into, through and out of your organisation. Such a data flow can include a transfer of information from one departement to another. However, this can also include the transfer between e.g. different locations, external partner organisations or suppliers.

To do that it is necessary to identify different key elements. This can include the data types and its format, its transfer method, its location and the clarification of the data accountability and data access. Such data mapping and data flows are important to be compliant with GDPR Article 30 and Article 36.

You can find more information about data mapping in GDPR here and here, if you are looking for a template you can find some here.

3.

Do you have documented what personal data you hold of your customers, employees, etc., where it came from, who you share it with and what you do with it?

Recommendation is missing.

Specification — Specific Subtitle?

The term "personal data" is in the center of GDPR, because it only applies if data processing concers personal data. Personal data contains any information which are related to an identified or identifiable natural person. According to GDPR a natural person or also called data subject is identifiable if they can be directly or indirectly identified through the data. This can e.g. be a name, an identification number, location data, number plate or any characteristics, which expresses the physical, physiological, genetic, mental, commercial, cultural or social identity of these persons.

You can find more information about personal data and its processing guidelines here and in Article 4 and Article 9 of GDPR.

To fullfil this task it is recommended to document the findings of the question before in an information asset register. In this register you must record data like the name and details of your organisation, the categories of the processing carried out on behalf of each controller, a description of technical and organisational security measures, etc. This provides an institutionwide view of all information assets and will provide insights to improve the management and security of the information. This allows a reasonable and proportionate approach to mitigate risks and minimize the effect of both cyber and business disruptions. You can find more information about the information asset register here and here. Further, here you can also find additional guidelines and a template of such a register.
