Controllers and Processors Checklist

First of all you should determine whether your organisation has the role of a data controller, a data processor or both. This clarification is important because the scope of requirements to achieve GDPR compliance is different for the two roles.

Data Controllers Role

The data controller is the person or organisation who determines the purpose, procedure and scope of data usage and processing. Data controllers hold the responsibility for protecting the privacy and rights of a data subject. This means that the data controller holds the control over the data. The data controller is also responsible for obtaining and managing tasks like obtaining the consent for data processing.

Controllers Checklist

Data Processors Role

The data processor processes data on behalf of the data controller. A data processor does neither own nor control the data processed. This means that the data processor is not able to change the purpose and means in which the data is used. The are bound to the instructions given by the data controller. In this context, data processing includes also trivial tasks such as data storing on a third party's servers.

Information Security Checklist

To be compliant with the GDPR guidelines, it is necessary that the existing and used IT infrastructure is secure and maintained. For this, it is important that you comply with data protection in the specific areas of information and cyber security policies and risks, mobile and home working, removable media, access controls and malware protection.

The security controls listed here show which information security best practices are required to support compliance with the EU GDPR and to manage the data of EU individuals in accordance with the GDPR.

Data Sharing and Subject Access Checklist

To be compliant with GDPR guidelines, it is necessary to have appropriate data sharing and subject access policies in place. In particular, it is of great importance that you yourself, but also your clients, are clear about the intentions of data sharing. Your clients have a right to know how their data is being used, i.e. what data is being collected, stored, processed and with whom it is being shared. The checklist is designed to help you assess your data sharing policies and agreements and monitor compliance.

Key points of data sharing are described in this use case. Chapter 3 of the GDPR policy also provides information on the rights of the data subject and, in the case of international data transfer, Chapter 5 should also be consulted.

Lawful Basis Checklist

You are only allowed to collect, store and process data if you have a lawful basis to do that. This reason makes the lawful basis to one of the most important topics in GDPR. GDPR knows the following six lawful bases:

  • Consent
  • Performance of a Contract
  • Legitimate Interest
  • Vital Interest
  • Legal Requirement
  • Public Interest


Choosing the correct and appropriate lawful basis is of utmost importance because it cannot be changed afterwards. However, this checklist will support you in choosing the correct lawful basis for your organisation. If you need more information about it, you can find some here, here and in Chapter 6 of the GDPR guidelines.
